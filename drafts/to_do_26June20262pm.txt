* **Global Hubs:** A few designated "summary" tokens are allowed to look at everything, acting as a bridge so distant parts of a document can still share context.
* **Dynamic Routing:** The model intelligently predicts which specific historical tokens are actually important to the current query and only connects to those, ignoring the irrelevant "filler" text.

**The Analogy:** Imagine reading a 1,000-page mystery novel. *Standard attention* requires you to cross-reference every new word you read with every single word in the previous 999 pages. *Sparse attention* is closer to how humans read: you pay deep attention to the current paragraph, remember a few major themes from previous chapters, and only perform a targeted search of earlier pages if a specific clue is brought up again.

* **Authors:** Jingyang Yuan, Huazuo Gao, Damai Dai, et al. (DeepSeek-AI & Peking University)
* **Why it matters:** This paper is the gold standard for NSA. It introduced a "dynamic hierarchical sparse strategy." Instead of calculating every token, it performs a fast, computationally light scan first, compressing tokens into temporal blocks. It then performs fine-grained attention *only* on the most important blocks, alongside a dense sliding window for immediate local memory. By avoiding non-differentiable math, it perfectly aligns with the hardware constraints of modern AI chips (Tensor Cores). It proved that a natively trained sparse model could maintain the exact same long-context reasoning as Full Attention but run dramatically faster.

### 2. SSA (Sparse Structured Attention)

"Structured" sparse attention avoids random sparsity (which can accidentally sever critical long-range connections) and instead forces the attention mechanism into mathematically principled, predictable geometric patterns.

* **The LLM Pioneers:** *[Longformer: The Long-Document Transformer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.05150)* (2020) and *[Big Bird: Transformers for Longer Sequences](https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.14062)* (Google, 2020)
* **Why they matter:** These two 2020 papers are the absolute bedrock of modern structured sparse attention. Google's *Big Bird* mathematically proved that combining three strict structures—a sliding local window, block-wise attention, and a few "global" summary tokens—was Turing Complete and could perfectly approximate dense attention, dropping computational complexity from a quadratic $O(N^2)$ to a linear $O(N)$.


* **The 2026 Subquadratic Frontier:** *[Structured-Sparse Attention for Entity Tracking with Subquadratic Sequence Complexity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22476)* (May 2026)
* **Authors:** Hangyue Zhao, Paul Caillon, Erwan Fagnou, Alexandre Allauzen.
* **Why it matters:** Representing the bleeding edge of SSA, this recent wave of research pushes structured sparsity even further. The authors proved that in massively long sequences, learned attention naturally clumps together in "block-diagonal neighborhoods" with very light cross-chatter. ### 3. Sparse Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)

While sparse *attention* limits which **words** look at each other, Sparse *MoE* limits which parts of the **AI's neural network** activate to process those words. It is the foundational backbone of models like Gemini, GPT-4, and DeepSeek, decoupling a model's parameter count from its computational cost.

* **The "Big Bang" Genesis Paper:** *[Outrageously Large Neural Networks: The Sparsely-Gated Mixture-of-Experts Layer](https://arxiv.org/abs/1701.06538)* (Google Brain, 2017)
* **Authors:** Noam Shazeer, Azalia Mirhoseini, et al.
* **Why it matters:** This is the paper that brought MoE into the deep learning era. * **The Trillion-Parameter Blueprint:** *[Switch Transformers: Scaling to Trillion Parameter Models with Simple and Efficient Sparsity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.03961)* (Google, 2021)
* **Authors:** William Fedus, Barret Zoph, Noam Shazeer.
* **Why it matters:** Early MoE models suffered from "expert collapse"—the router would just send every token to the same few experts and ignore the rest. * **The Open-Weight Standard:** *[Mixtral of Experts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.04088)* (Mistral AI, Jan 2024)
* **Authors:** Albert Q. Jiang, et al.
* **Why it matters:** This paper brought MoE out of Big Tech's private labs. This precise Top-2 sparse routing proved that an MoE model could vastly outperform dense models three times its size while running at a fraction of the cost, making it the default template for modern open-weight AI development.
